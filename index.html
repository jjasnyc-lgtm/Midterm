Project Overview

The Harlem Plaza midterm redesign addresses urban safety, accessibility, comfort, and community engagement. The project integrates AI-generated visuals to support design decisions.

The goal is to create a public space that promotes walking, recreation, events, and social interaction while considering local context and user needs.

Site Analysis

  • Current traffic patterns and pedestrian flow
  • Safety hazards and blind spots in the plaza
  • Existing green spaces and seating availability
  • Business activity and event spaces
  • Community accessibility and inclusion needs

AI-assisted mapping tools were used to analyze patterns and propose areas for improvement.

Design Goals

  • Enhance pedestrian movement and comfort
  • Increase safety with better lighting and visibility
  • Expand green spaces and seating areas
  • Encourage community interaction and events
  • Use AI-assisted modeling to test designs

Community Impact

The redesign prioritizes safety, inclusivity, and comfort. Stakeholders and local community members were considered during the planning process.

  • Accessible spaces for all ages and abilities
  • Spaces for social events and cultural activities
  • Optimized pedestrian flow to reduce congestion
  • Increased greenery to improve air quality and comfort

Reflection

This project illustrates how AI-assisted design can support urban planning decisions. By combining analysis, community needs, and AI-generated visuals, the redesign addresses practical and aesthetic concerns.

The midterm demonstrates the value of iterative design, research, and integrating technology into urban planning projects.
